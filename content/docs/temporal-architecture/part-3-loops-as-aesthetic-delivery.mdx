# Part 3: Loops as Aesthetic Delivery

Different aesthetic goals require fundamentally different temporal structures. This isn't a matter of preference or style—it's about what each aesthetic needs to function. A Discovery game with repetitive loops betrays its purpose. A Mastery game with constantly changing rules frustrates learning. A Submission game with time pressure contradicts relaxation.

This section maps aesthetic goals to loop requirements, providing validated patterns you can adapt to your specific design. We're not prescribing exact implementations—we're showing what each aesthetic needs from its temporal architecture.

## Discovery Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Exploration, finding new things, understanding systems, revealing secrets

### Loop Requirements

**Variety at Each Iteration**

* Micro loops must reveal new information or present different situations
* Meso loops should open new areas, mechanics, or narrative beats
* Macro loops should fundamentally change understanding or capabilities
* Repetition without variety kills Discovery—each cycle must add something new

**Gating that Creates Anticipation**

* Visible locked content creates "what's behind that door?" pull
* Equipment/ability gates create clear progress markers
* Knowledge gates (need to understand X to access Y) reward attention
* Artificial gates (timers, energy) contradict Discovery—use diegetic gates

**Reveals that Recontextualize**

* Information discovered should change understanding of earlier content
* "Aha!" moments where pieces click together
* Secrets that reward thorough exploration
* Environmental storytelling that unfolds gradually

### Successful Pattern: Outer Wilds

**Micro loop (30-60 seconds):** Explore location → find clue → update ship log

* Each location teaches something new
* Immediate feedback (log updates with new knowledge)
* No two locations feel identical

**Meso loop (5-15 minutes):** Piece together planetary mystery → discover connection → new question emerges

* Each planet has distinct puzzle
* Solving one mystery reveals deeper mysteries
* No repetition—each visit teaches something new

**Macro loop (22 minutes):** Time loop forces new approach each cycle

* Time constraint prevents exhaustive exploration in one loop
* Must synthesize knowledge across multiple loops
* Death isn't failure—it's "now I know, let's try something else"

**Meta loop (15-25 hours):** Understanding the full mystery → multiple "aha!" moments

* Each revelation recontextualizes earlier discoveries
* Final understanding changes perception of everything
* No repetitive grinding—pure knowledge accumulation

**Why it works:** Every loop scale emphasizes variety and revelation. No two cycles feel the same. Knowledge is the only progression—no power scaling, no resource accumulation. Pure Discovery aesthetic.

### Successful Pattern: Metroidvanias (Hollow Knight)

**Micro loop (30-60 seconds):** Explore room → encounter new enemy type/hazard → learn pattern

* Environments vary constantly (biomes, layouts, challenges)
* Combat variety through enemy diversity
* Movement variety through terrain complexity

**Meso loop (10-30 minutes):** Explore area → find upgrade → access previously blocked areas

* Each upgrade meaningfully changes traversal options
* Backtracking reveals new paths (not repetition, revelation)
* Boss encounters punctuate exploration

**Macro loop (2-4 hours):** Complete region → unlock major ability → entire map opens up

* Significant capability increases (double jump, wall climb, dash)
* Old areas become accessible in new ways
* Secrets reward thorough exploration

**Meta loop (30-60 hours):** Map completion, secret discovery, mastery of movement

* Optional challenges for dedicated explorers
* Speedrun potential for mastery players
* World reveals depth on repeat playthroughs

**Why it works:** Equipment gates create anticipation. Ability unlocks change movement fundamentally (not just numbers). Backtracking discovers new content, not repetition. Micro variety through enemy/environment diversity.

### Discovery Anti-Pattern: Early No Man's Sky

**Problem:** Procedural generation without meaningful variation

* 18 quintillion planets, but all feel similar after first 10
* Micro loop (mining) identical everywhere
* Meso loop (explore planet) no variety—same resources, same structures
* Discovery promise broken by repetition

**Lesson:** Procedural generation must create genuine variety, not cosmetic differences. Better to have 50 hand-crafted locations than infinite similar ones.

## Challenge Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Overcoming difficulty through skill development, earning victory through mastery of systems

### Loop Requirements

**Clear Failure/Success States**

* Micro loops must have unambiguous outcomes (hit or miss, success or fail)
* No "you kind of succeeded"—binary or clearly graduated results
* Failure must provide information for next attempt
* Success must feel earned, not lucky

**Skill Progression Separate from Power Progression**

* Player gets better at execution, not just character gets stronger
* Same challenge should become easier through practice
* Pattern recognition and muscle memory matter
* Victory condition: player skill, not time investment

**Failure that Teaches**

* Death/failure reveals what went wrong
* Patterns emerge through repeated attempts
* Each failure provides specific lesson
* "I know what to do differently next time"

### Successful Pattern: Sekiro

**Micro loop (1-3 seconds):** Attack → deflect → counter

* Instant feedback on success/failure (sound, visual, posture bar)
* Timing windows are learnable (consistent, not random)
* Mastery creates flow (deflect perfectly without thinking)

**Meso loop (5-15 minutes):** Learn boss patterns → execute strategy → defeat phase

* Each boss has learnable pattern
* Failure reveals which pattern you don't know yet
* Success requires executing learned patterns under pressure

**Macro loop (30-90 minutes):** Defeat boss → progress story → unlock new area

* Boss is skill gate (can't progress without mastery)
* Victory is pure achievement (minimal power scaling)
* New area introduces new challenges

**Meta loop (20-40 hours):** Master combat system → defeat all bosses → achieve mastery

* Optional challenges for mastery demonstration
* Speedrun community emerges organically
* New Game+ for mastery expression

**Why it works:** Micro loops are tight and consistent (enables pattern learning). Failure teaches specific lessons. Victory is earned through skill, not grinding. Power progression minimal—player improvement is primary.

### Successful Pattern: Celeste

**Micro loop (1-5 seconds):** Precise jump → land safely → next obstacle

* Pixel-perfect platforming with consistent physics
* Instant respawn after death (no punishment)
* Muscle memory develops through repetition

**Meso loop (5-20 minutes):** Complete screen → checkpoint → next screen

* Each screen is self-contained challenge
* Checkpoints prevent frustration (progress preserved)
* Difficulty ramps smoothly

**Macro loop (30-90 minutes):** Complete chapter → story beat → new mechanic introduced

* Major sense of achievement at chapter complete
* Story reinforces "you can do hard things" message
* New mechanics keep challenges fresh

**Meta loop (10-30 hours):** Complete main story → B-sides → C-sides → Chapter 9

* Optional difficulty for mastery seekers
* Core story completable by most players
* Hardcore content available but not required

**Why it works:** Accessibility features (assist mode) separate story access from challenge mastery. Challenge players get pure skill tests. Story players get narrative. Both valid approaches to same content.

### Challenge Anti-Pattern: Anthem

**Problem:** Unclear what success requires

* Loot is random (luck not skill)
* Difficulty spikes unpredictable (not learnable)
* Victory doesn't feel earned (was that skill or gear?)
* Bullet sponge enemies (tedium not challenge)

**Lesson:** Challenge requires predictable difficulty and clear skill requirements. Random loot contradicts skill-based progression.

## Expression Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Creativity, self-expression, building/creating something unique

### Loop Requirements

**Build/Test/Iterate Cycles**

* Must be able to create something
* Must be able to test it immediately
* Must be able to refine based on testing
* Iteration should be low-friction

**Save and Share Mechanisms**

* Creations must persist
* Must be able to show others
* Community feedback loop valuable
* No "wrong" outcomes—all expression valid

**No Failure States (or Failure is Part of Creation)**

* Can't "lose" at building
* Constraints create interesting problems, not failures
* Testing might reveal issues, but that's information not failure

### Successful Pattern: Mario Maker

**Micro loop (5-30 seconds):** Place block → test jump → adjust placement

* Instant feedback (place and test immediately)
* Undo is trivial (experimentation encouraged)
* Physics consistent (learning transfers)

**Meso loop (10-60 minutes):** Build section → playtest thoroughly → refine until satisfied

* Self-contained creation chunk
* Can publish partial levels for feedback
* Iteration is core gameplay

**Macro loop (1-4 hours):** Complete level → upload → receive plays/feedback

* Clear completion point (level publishable)
* Community engagement (plays, comments, stars)
* Success measured by creator satisfaction and player reception

**Meta loop (ongoing):** Build level catalog → develop style → gain following

* Reputation building through quality
* Learning from other creators
* Identity as "maker" emerges

**Why it works:** Build/test/iterate cycle is frictionless. Community provides feedback and motivation. No way to "fail"—all levels are valid expressions. Constraints (item limits, space) create interesting problems.

### Successful Pattern: Minecraft Creative

**Micro loop (1-5 seconds):** Place block → see result → place next block

* Instant feedback (block appears)
* Infinite resources (no scarcity friction)
* Undo easily (break and replace)

**Meso loop (10-60 minutes):** Build structure component → step back and evaluate → refine

* Natural pause points for evaluation
* No time pressure
* Save anytime

**Macro loop (hours to days):** Complete build → share screenshot/tour → start new project

* Self-defined completion
* Social sharing optional but supported
* Projects can be abandoned without loss

**Meta loop (ongoing):** Develop building skills → tackle more ambitious projects → join communities

* Skill progression through practice
* Inspiration from other builders
* Optional social/multiplayer building

**Why it works:** No constraints beyond space. Creative mode removes survival friction. Building is inherently satisfying. Community provides inspiration and validation without pressure.

## Mastery Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Deep understanding, optimization, perfect execution, demonstrating expertise

### Loop Requirements

**Consistent Rules to Internalize**

* Systems must be deterministic (same input → same output)
* Complexity is fine, but must be learnable
* Hidden information contradicts mastery (need to know rules to master them)
* Meta should not change core systems (consistency enables mastery)

**Depth that Reveals Over Time**

* Simple surface, complex depth (easy to learn, hard to master)
* Multiple layers of optimization
* Skill ceiling extremely high
* Always something new to learn

**Skill Differentiation**

* Clear difference between novice and expert execution
* Mastery should be visible to others
* Systems should support competitive play or clear benchmarks
* Rankings/achievements for mastery demonstration

### Successful Pattern: Rocket League

**Micro loop (1-3 seconds):** Hit ball → control car → position for next hit

* Physics consistent (enables skill development)
* Input → output relationship learnable
* Ceiling extremely high (freestyle aerials, flip resets)

**Meso loop (5-10 minutes):** Score goals → win match → rank reflects performance

* Each match tests current skill level
* Matchmaking ensures appropriate challenge
* Immediate feedback on performance

**Macro loop (hours per session):** Climb ranked ladder → reach new tier → face better opponents

* Progression tied to skill, not time
* Tiers provide clear milestones
* Can play casually or competitively

**Meta loop (hundreds to thousands of hours):** Master advanced techniques → reach high ranks → join competitive community

* Skill progression never caps
* Community respects mastery
* Speedruns, freestyle competitions, tournaments

**Why it works:** Simple core (hit ball with car), infinite depth (air control, positioning, team coordination). Consistent physics enable mastery. Ranked system provides progression and challenge. Community values skill demonstration.

### Successful Pattern: Into the Breach

**Micro loop (30-120 seconds):** Analyze turn → plan moves → preview outcomes → execute

* Perfect information (can see exact results)
* Deterministic (no randomness in outcomes)
* Undo encourages experimentation (find optimal solution)

**Meso loop (5-10 minutes):** Complete mission → achieve objectives → earn stars

* Perfect completion possible (3-star rating)
* Multiple solutions (optimization possible)
* Clear success metrics

**Macro loop (60-90 minutes):** Complete island campaign → pilot saved → unlock rewards

* Victory through mastery of systems
* Difficulty options for challenge seeking
* Achievements for specific mastery feats

**Meta loop (30-100 hours):** Master all squads → complete all difficulties → perfect runs

* Each squad plays completely differently (replayability through variety)
* Higher difficulties require deeper mastery
* Community shares optimal strategies

**Why it works:** Perfect information removes frustration, enables optimization. Deterministic outcomes reward planning. Undo system encourages finding best solution. Each squad requires mastering different systems.

## Fellowship Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Social connection, cooperation, shared achievement, playing together

### Loop Requirements

**Coordination Opportunities**

* Mechanics that require or benefit from coordination
* Communication channels (voice, text, pings)
* Roles that complement each other
* Shared objectives that require cooperation

**Low Mechanical Complexity (Often)**

* Complex execution can prevent conversation
* Social focus means mechanics should be secondary
* Exception: Coordinated complexity (raid bosses) works if team is dedicated

**Shared Success and Failure**

* Team wins together or loses together
* Individual performance matters but doesn't overshadow team
* Carrying or being carried should both feel okay
* Comeback mechanics preserve engagement for losing team

### Successful Pattern: Deep Rock Galactic

**Micro loop (5-30 seconds):** Spot threat → call out → team responds → threat eliminated

* Communication is gameplay (pings, voice)
* Classes complement (scout lights, driller tunnels, engineer platforms, gunner firepower)
* No "optimal" play—many solutions work

**Meso loop (15-30 minutes):** Complete mission objective → extract together → share rewards

* Must coordinate extraction (can't leave anyone behind)
* Shared reward pool (no competition for loot)
* Success requires cooperation (solo is much harder)

**Macro loop (1-3 hours):** Complete multiple missions → upgrade equipment → unlock cosmetics

* Progression shared across whole team
* Cosmetics show dedication but don't create power gaps
* Beer drinking between missions (social time)

**Meta loop (hundreds of hours):** Build regular team → tackle harder difficulties → develop group identity

* "Rock and Stone!" culture (positive community)
* Difficulty scales for dedicated teams
* No FOMO—play at your pace

**Why it works:** Cooperation is mechanically required (classes need each other). Low toxicity (PvE, shared rewards). Voice lines encourage positive culture ("Rock and Stone!"). Can play casually or hardcore.

### Successful Pattern: Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes

**Micro loop (10-30 seconds):** Describe bomb module → consult manual → provide instructions → defuse

* Pure cooperation (one person can't solve alone)
* Communication is core mechanic
* Extremely low mechanical complexity (click buttons, flip switches)

**Meso loop (3-10 minutes):** Defuse bomb → timer adds pressure → success or explosion

* Clear shared goal (bomb defused)
* Escalating difficulty (more modules, less time)
* Failure is funny (explosion, not tragedy)

**Macro loop (30-90 minutes):** Multiple bombs → increasing complexity → group learning

* Team gets better together
* Inside jokes develop ("NOT THE BLUE WIRE!")
* Social bonding through shared challenges

**Meta loop:** Group game night tradition, rotating players, teaching new people

* No persistent progression needed
* Social context drives return (party game)
* Works with different player combinations

**Why it works:** Zero mechanical skill required (all about communication). Asymmetric information creates dependency. Pressure (timer) creates excitement. Failure is funny, success is triumphant. Perfect party game.

## Submission Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Relaxation, flow without pressure, "turning brain off," low-stakes enjoyment

### Loop Requirements

**Low Cognitive Load Throughout**

* Simple mechanics that become automatic
* Minimal decision complexity
* No time pressure (or gentle pressure only)
* Forgiving failure (or no failure states)

**Gentle, Predictable Rhythm**

* Soothing repetition, not tedious repetition
* Clear patterns, no surprises
* Optional complexity (can engage deeper if desired)
* No sudden difficulty spikes

**No Obligation or Pressure**

* No FOMO mechanics
* No competitive elements (unless very casual)
* No daily requirements
* Can stop anytime without penalty

### Successful Pattern: Vampire Survivors

**Micro loop (continuous):** Move → enemies die automatically → XP pickups appear

* Zero mechanical complexity (just move)
* Automatic progression (don't have to think)
* Constant positive feedback (enemies dying, XP accumulating)

**Meso loop (60-90 seconds):** Level up → choose upgrade → return to flow

* Brief strategic moment (choose but not stressed)
* Immediate return to flow state
* Choices matter but aren't punishing

**Macro loop (15-30 minutes):** Run completes (death or 30-min timer) → see what unlocked

* Hard time cap prevents "just one more" trap
* Always get something (unlocks, currency)
* Death feels like completion, not failure

**Meta loop (20-60 hours):** Unlock characters/weapons → try new builds → complete achievements

* Intrinsic motivation (curiosity about builds)
* No daily pressure
* Can stop for weeks and return easily

**Why it works:** Cognitive load near-zero at micro scale. No execution requirements. Flow state maintained for 30 minutes. Hard cap respects player time. No FOMO or obligation.

### Successful Pattern: Animal Crossing: New Horizons

**Micro loop (10-60 seconds):** Gather resources → talk to villagers → arrange items

* Simple, low-pressure actions
* Everything moves at player's pace
* No fail states (can't do anything "wrong")

**Meso loop (10-30 minutes):** Complete daily tasks → check shops → work on project

* Self-directed goals (player chooses focus)
* No urgency (can do tomorrow)
* Always gentle progress

**Macro loop (30-90 minutes):** Make visible island progress → feel satisfied → natural stopping point

* Easy to stop anytime (auto-save)
* No penalty for stopping
* Session feels complete even if goals unfinished

**Meta loop (months):** Seasonal events → island development → collection completion

* Real-time progression (can't rush)
* Gentle FOMO (events come back yearly—missing one isn't permanent)
* Community sharing optional but encouraged

**Why it works:** No time pressure. No failure. Can play 10 minutes or 3 hours. Real-time mechanics prevent binging. Gentle goals without obligation. Social (visiting islands) optional.

## Narrative Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Story engagement, character development, emotional moments, plot progression

### Loop Requirements

**Story Beats at Appropriate Scales**

* Meso loops should contain story moments or character development
* Macro loops should resolve story arcs or major questions
* Meta loops should provide overarching narrative structure
* Micro loops should not interrupt narrative flow

**Pacing that Matches Dramatic Structure**

* Build tension → climax → resolution → breathing room
* Quiet character moments between action
* Player agency in story where appropriate
* Authored critical path with exploration freedom

**Low Mechanical Friction**

* Complex mechanics can distract from story
* Combat/challenges should serve narrative, not overshadow it
* Difficulty options so story access isn't gated by skill
* Mechanics that reinforce themes

### Successful Pattern: The Last of Us

**Micro loop (30-120 seconds):** Stealth/combat encounter → progress → environmental storytelling

* Mechanics simple enough to not distract from narrative
* Environmental details tell story during gameplay
* Encounters punctuate story beats, don't dominate

**Meso loop (20-45 minutes):** Story sequence → gameplay section → next story beat

* Clear dramatic structure (setup → tension → resolution)
* Gameplay reinforces narrative themes (survival, protection)
* Cutscenes and gameplay flow together

**Macro loop (2-4 hours):** Complete chapter → major story development → emotional beat

* Each chapter has arc (beginning, middle, end)
* Character relationships develop
* Player feels narrative progress

**Meta loop (12-20 hours):** Complete story → process emotional journey → discuss themes

* Defined ending (complete story)
* Emotional payoff from investment
* Story stays with players after completion

**Why it works:** Mechanics serve story (stealth/survival matches themes). Difficulty options separate story access from challenge. Strong characters carry emotional weight. Pacing allows breathing room between intense moments.

### Successful Pattern: Disco Elysium

**Micro loop (1-5 minutes):** Read dialogue → make choice → see consequence

* Mechanics are primarily reading and choosing
* No combat to distract from narrative
* Choices feel meaningful immediately

**Meso loop (20-60 minutes):** Investigate scene → gather clues → advance case

* Detective work is narrative exploration
* Each location tells story through detail
* Skills provide different narrative perspectives

**Macro loop (3-6 hours):** Complete day → major case development → reflect on choices

* Clear day structure provides pacing
* Choices accumulate consequences
* Character development through internal dialogue

**Meta loop (20-40 hours):** Solve case → understand character → process themes

* Multiple endings based on choices
* Political themes explored through gameplay
* Replayability through different builds revealing different story aspects

**Why it works:** Mechanics ARE narrative (no separation). Skill system reveals character personality. Writing quality carries experience. No mechanical barriers to story access.

## Tension Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Adrenaline, time pressure, high-stakes decisions, nail-biting moments

### Loop Requirements

**Risk Frequency and Consequence**

* Constant threat of failure
* Mistakes should matter
* Success should feel like relief, not triumph
* Recovery slow (consequences persist)

**Interrupted Flow**

* Unlike Submission (sustained flow), Tension breaks comfort
* Moments of calm make tension spikes more effective
* Never let player feel safe for long
* Punctuated pacing (calm → sudden threat → barely survive)

**Time Pressure or Scarcity**

* Limited resources force difficult choices
* Time limits create urgency
* Can't solve problems by being careful—must act under pressure
* Paranoia is feature (always checking corners, listening)

### Successful Pattern: Alien: Isolation

**Micro loop (10-60 seconds):** Hide → wait → carefully move → hide again

* Constant threat (alien can appear anytime)
* Sound cues create paranoia
* One mistake often fatal

**Meso loop (10-30 minutes):** Reach objective while avoiding alien → barely survive → temporary safety

* Limited resources (supplies scarce)
* Backtracking through known threats
* Safe rooms provide brief relief

**Macro loop (2-4 hours):** Complete station area → unlock new area → new threats emerge

* Progression feels like going deeper into danger
* Alien becomes smarter (learns your patterns)
* Each area has its own tension cycle

**Meta loop (15-25 hours):** Survive Sevastopol → escape → cathartic relief

* Sustained tension across hours
* Learning alien behavior (mastery helps but doesn't remove threat)
* Ending provides resolution

**Why it works:** Threat is constant and unpredictable. Resources scarce (careful planning required). Alien AI creates genuine fear (learns, adapts). Safe rooms provide necessary relief. Sound design creates paranoia.

### Successful Pattern: FTL: Faster Than Light

**Micro loop (1-5 seconds):** Pause → assign crew → target systems → unpause → pray

* Every decision under pressure (rebels approaching)
* Pause allows tactical thought without execution pressure
* Consequences immediate and visible

**Meso loop (5-15 minutes):** Jump through sector → encounters → upgrade ship

* Resource scarcity (never enough scrap)
* Every choice matters (miss upgrade now, might die later)
* RNG creates unpredictability (can't rely on plans)

**Macro loop (30-90 minutes):** Reach final sector → fight flagship → probably die

* Permadeath raises stakes
* Each run feels desperate
* Victory rare, defeat teaches

**Meta loop (20-60 hours):** Unlock ships → master strategies → eventually win

* High difficulty creates sustained tension
* Unlocks provide progression despite frequent death
* Mastery emerges through repeated attempts

**Why it works:** Time pressure (rebels chasing). Resource scarcity (tough choices). Permadeath (consequences permanent). RNG creates unpredictability. Pause-to-plan removes execution pressure while maintaining strategic pressure.

## Progression Aesthetic

**Core Experience:** Growth, becoming more powerful, visible advancement, unlocking capabilities

### Loop Requirements

**Measurable Advancement**

* Clear metrics (levels, stats, unlocks)
* Visible power increase (new capabilities or numbers)
* Regular milestones (new tier, new area, new powers)
* Can't just be cosmetic—must affect gameplay

**Sense of Growth Over Time**

* Early game should feel weak
* Mid game should feel competent
* Late game should feel powerful
* Contrast creates satisfaction

**Multiple Progression Systems (Often)**

* Character levels
* Equipment upgrades
* Skill unlocks
* Map completion
* Multiple tracks provide constant progression

### Successful Pattern: Hades

**Micro loop:** Combat feels slightly better each run (weapon mastery)
**Meso loop:** Boon combinations create power spikes during run
**Macro loop:** Death provides currencies for permanent upgrades

* Mirror of Night (permanent stat increases)
* Weapon upgrades (new aspects unlock)
* Story progression (relationships deepen)

**Meta loop:** Unlock all weapons → max all relationships → complete prophecies

* Hundreds of permanent upgrades
* Story gated by runs (need to die to progress)
* Heat system adds challenge for mastery players

**Why it works:** Death is progress (always get currency). Permanent upgrades make runs easier (but skill still matters). Story and power progression intertwined. Multiple progression tracks (combat, story, relationships) always have something advancing.

## Investment and Payoff by Aesthetic

Different aesthetics have different investment-to-payoff relationships:

**High Investment, Deep Payoff:**

* **Mastery**: Hundreds of hours to truly master, but satisfaction from expertise is profound
* **Discovery**: Mental investment in understanding systems, payoff from "aha!" moments
* **Challenge**: Time investment learning patterns, payoff from overcoming difficulty

**Low Investment, Immediate Payoff:**

* **Submission**: Minimal cognitive load, immediate relaxation and flow
* **Sensation**: Direct sensory pleasure, instant gratification
* **Fellowship**: Social connection, immediate fun with friends

**Medium Investment, Growing Payoff:**

* **Narrative**: Initial time investment, growing emotional engagement
* **Progression**: Regular time investment, constant sense of advancement
* **Expression**: Creative investment, satisfaction grows with skill

**Match your loop architecture to your aesthetic's investment-payoff profile:**

* High investment aesthetics need long macro loops (Civilization for Mastery)
* Low investment aesthetics need short macro loops (Vampire Survivors for Submission)
* Medium investment aesthetics need clear session chunks (Hades for Progression + Narrative)

## Configuration Coherence Test: Temporal Edition

From Chapter 1, we learned to ask: "Do all five components serve the same aesthetic?"

Now extend that to temporal architecture:

**Questions to ask:**

1. Do micro loops create the right flow profile for my aesthetic?
2. Do meso loops provide appropriate goals (variety for Discovery, consistency for Mastery)?
3. Do macro loops match player capacity and provide closure?
4. Do meta loops use intrinsic motivation aligned with aesthetic?
5. Does investment trajectory match aesthetic requirements?

**Example: Hades (Coherent)**

* ✅ Micro: Fast combat (Challenge)
* ✅ Meso: Boon variety (Discovery) + build optimization (Mastery)
* ✅ Macro: Death = story progress (Narrative) + power increase (Progression)
* ✅ Meta: Relationships (Narrative) + weapon mastery (Mastery + Challenge)
* All loops reinforce Challenge + Narrative + Progression + Discovery

**Example: Destiny 2 (Incoherent at Meta Scale)**

* ✅ Micro: Excellent gunplay (Challenge)
* ✅ Meso: Team activities (Fellowship + Challenge)
* ✅ Macro: Weekly milestones... wait, this creates obligation
* ❌ Meta: FOMO seasons, power treadmill (serves business model, not aesthetics)
* Micro/meso serve aesthetics, but meta-loop breaks coherence

**Red flags for incoherence:**

* Micro loops for Mastery (consistent) but meso loops for Discovery (variety) without integration
* Challenge aesthetic with Submission-style loops (no failure states)
* Discovery aesthetic with repetitive loops (variety missing)
* Meta loops creating obligation when aesthetic promises relaxation
