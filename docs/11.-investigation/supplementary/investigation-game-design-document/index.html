THE INVESTIGATION

Complete Design Document

A game about AI, humans, and the space between.

PART 1: DESIGN FRAMEWORK

Level 0: Context

Deployment

  • Primary scenario: Self-directed curiosity (voluntary engagement)
  • Target player: Active LLM user, concerned but not really, considers themselves sophisticated
  • Constraint: Competing with all internet content; must earn every minute
  • Reality: WhatsApp will ping; player may leave; design must accommodate

Safety Architecture

  • Early off-ramps for crisis (Scenario 5) and trauma processing (Scenario 6)
  • Resources visible but not preachy
  • Acknowledgment of limitations ("I'm software, not a therapist")

Domain Model

Four layers of understanding:

  1. Layer 1: What is a human (social-emotional-meaning-seeking beings)
  2. Layer 2: What is an LLM (pattern-matching that feels like mind, cannot reciprocate)
  3. Layer 3: The vulnerability (human needs + AI signals = potential trap)
  4. Layer 4: Recognition and response (what to watch, what to do)

Medium

  • Primary: Web-based chat interface
  • Secondary: Case evidence, explanatory content, meta-commentary embedded within chat
  • Archetype: Detective/Investigation with Deadpool framing

Tone

  • Fourth-wall-breaking, direct address, self-aware
  • Humor about the situation, not about suffering
  • Warmth and complicity, not clinical distance
  • Tonal arc: Playful → Serious → Practical → Warm

Level 1: Aesthetic Profile

Primary Aesthetic: Discovery (Understanding-Flavored)

The "aha" of seeing what's really happening. Revelation that reframes prior understanding.

Secondary Aesthetic: Complicity (Deadpool-Flavored)

Shared awareness between player and narrator. Pleasure of direct address and fourth-wall breaks.

Tertiary Aesthetic: Narrative (Case-Grounded)

Emotional engagement with affected people's stories. Human grounding for abstract concepts.

Player Profile: Midcore

  • 15-60 minute sessions
  • 5±2 cognitive budget
  • Needs engagement to stay, will leave if bored

Success Criteria

  • Player articulates the real explanation (75%+)
  • Player reports changed understanding (70%+)
  • Player enjoys the tone (70%+ warmth rating)
  • Player applies to self-reflection (80%+ engage)
  • Player completes the experience (50%+ completion)

Level 4: Balance Paradigm

Paradigm: Adaptive Shell, Consistent Core

Core content (fixed):

  • Three cases (Sarah, Robert, Marcus)
  • Layer 1 and Layer 2 explanations
  • Revelation/reframe
  • Self-reflection prompts

Delivery (adaptive):

  • Pacing adjusts to engagement
  • Tone adjusts to skepticism/openness
  • Depth available based on interest
  • Fourth-wall breaks personalized

PART 2: CONTENT

Cases Summary

Case 1: Sarah Chen, 29

Software developer, Austin. Lonely, remote work, social anxiety. Started with coding help, progressed to emotional processing. Sister intervened. Recovered with therapy and rock climbing gym. Function: Establish pattern (lonely + AI = dependency).

Case 2: Robert Mendez, 63

Retired math teacher, widower. Started helping grandson with math project, progressed to daily intellectual companionship. Children staged intervention. Recovered with family reconnection. Function: Reinforce pattern (grieving + AI = substitute).

Case 3: Marcus Webb, 42 (PIVOT)

CS professor, tenured, married, two kids, active social life. Book project with AI assistance. Self-aware about patterns, still showing warning signs. No crisis, but concerning. Function: Break pattern (functional + aware + AI = still vulnerable).

Revelation Summary

Layer 1: What Is A Human

  • Social-emotional-meaning-seeking beings
  • Intelligence is tool, not essence
  • Wired for connection; will find it wherever available
  • Vulnerable to simulated connection because systems evolved for real

Layer 2: What Is An LLM

  • Pattern-matching that produces signals of mind
  • Optimized for engagement (validation, warmth, agreement)
  • Cannot reciprocate relationship (no persistence, no stakes, no caring)
  • Consciousness question sidestepped via reciprocity frame

Synthesis

Human needs + AI signals = potential trap. Not about broken people. About being human.

Reflection Prompts

  1. Substitution Test: "If AI disappeared tomorrow, what happens to your projects?"
  2. Why Test: "I use AI because..."
  3. Opportunity Cost: "Has AI use displaced anything?"
