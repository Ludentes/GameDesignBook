Potential Coherence Risks

Risk 1: Duration Creep

Symptom: Average completion exceeds 55 minutes

Diagnosis: Parameters (word count) may be too high for temporal target

Mitigation:

  • Reduce depth content first
  • Then reduce per-case content
  • Monitor early playtests closely

Risk 2: Complicity Failure

Symptom: Players report feeling lectured (>15%)

Diagnosis: Narrator voice may be too directive; fourth-wall breaks may not land

Mitigation:

  • Increase acknowledgment language
  • Reduce claim density in revelation
  • Test narrator voice variants

Risk 3: Pivot Not Landing

Symptom: Players don't identify Case 3 as significant (<50%)

Diagnosis: Hypothesis crystallization may be too weak; Case 3 contrast may be insufficient

Mitigation:

  • Strengthen hypothesis prompt in Act 1 close
  • Increase contrast in Marcus's protective factors
  • Make "what makes you different?" more pointed

Risk 4: Self-Application Resistance

Symptom: <70% engage with reflection prompts

Diagnosis: Prompts may feel too personal/threatening; value may be unclear

Mitigation:

  • Strengthen transition framing ("this isn't diagnosis")
  • Make prompts more abstract/hypothetical if needed
  • Test prompt wording variants

Risk 5: Abandonment Before Pivot

Symptom: >35% drop off before Act 2

Diagnosis: Act 1 may not create sufficient pull; value unclear

Mitigation:

  • Strengthen hook
  • Add more Complicity signals in Act 1
  • Ensure each case delivers standalone value

Playtesting Priorities

Based on coherence audit, test in this order:

  1. Completion funnel — Where do people drop off?
  2. Revelation articulation — Can completers explain the reframe?
  3. Narrator warmth — Does Complicity land?
  4. Pivot recognition — Is Case 3 the significant one?
  5. Reflection engagement — Do people engage with self-application?

Sign-Off

Design complete through Level 7 + Coherence Audit.

Next steps:

  1. Implementation (build the interactive experience)
  2. Internal testing (team plays through)
  3. External testing (target audience)
  4. Iteration (based on validation predictions)

The design is ready for implementation.
