Balance Supplement 1: Measurables Catalog

Document Purpose

This catalog provides concrete target values for common measurables across different game types, aesthetics, and paradigms. These are starting points, not absolute rules—your specific game will require tuning based on playtesting.

How to use this catalog:

  1. Find your aesthetic + paradigm combination
  2. Identify relevant measurables for your game type
  3. Use target values as initial parameters
  4. Playtest and adjust ±20% as needed
  5. Iterate toward your specific experience

Confidence note: Values derived from analysis of successful games and industry best practices. Treat as informed guidelines requiring validation for your specific context.

Part 1: Challenge Aesthetic Measurables

Challenge + Consistency Balance

Game Type: Action Boss Fight (Dark Souls, Hollow Knight, Furi)

Time-to-Kill (TTK)

  • First-time player, successful attempt: 2-5 minutes
  • Experienced player (post-mastery): 1-3 minutes
  • Expert speedrunner: 30-90 seconds
  • Why these ranges: Long enough for pattern expression, short enough to retry without frustration

Attempts to First Victory

  • Casual difficulty: 3-8 attempts
  • Normal difficulty: 8-15 attempts
  • Hard difficulty: 15-30 attempts
  • Why these ranges: Sufficient struggle for satisfaction, not so many that players quit

Learning Curve Slope

  • TTK improvement: 15-25% from attempt 1 to attempt 10
  • Error rate reduction: 40-60% from early to late attempts
  • Pattern recognition: 70%+ prediction accuracy by attempt 5-7
  • Why these ranges: Clear progression signals learning is working

Failure Recovery Time

  • Death to retry: <10 seconds (ideal: <5 seconds)
  • Checkpoint to boss: <30 seconds run
  • Loading screens: <3 seconds
  • Why these ranges: Fast retry enables iteration without breaking flow

Pattern Readability

  • Attack telegraphs: 0.5-2 seconds warning
  • Distinct attack animations: 3-7 different patterns (learnable, not overwhelming)
  • Consistent timing: ±10% variance maximum
  • Why these ranges: Enough warning to react, consistent enough to learn

Game Type: Roguelike Combat Encounter (Slay the Spire, Hades, Dead Cells)

Time-to-Kill (Single Encounter)

  • Standard enemy room: 30-90 seconds
  • Elite encounter: 90-180 seconds
  • Boss fight: 3-8 minutes
  • Why these ranges: Multiple encounters per run, each must be quick enough for pacing

Death Rate by Stage

  • Early game (first 20%): 10-20% deaths per attempt
  • Mid game (middle 50%): 20-40% deaths per attempt
  • Late game (final 30%): 30-60% deaths per attempt
  • Why these ranges: Increasing difficulty provides progression curve

Run Duration

  • Successful run: 20-45 minutes
  • Failed run: 5-30 minutes (depending on progress)
  • Meta unlock grind: 50-200 runs to full completion
  • Why these ranges: Respects player time while providing replayability

Build Diversity

  • Viable builds: 8-15 distinct strategies
  • Winrate variance: <20% between viable builds
  • Dominant strategy: No build should exceed 65% winrate
  • Why these ranges: Multiple paths to victory without perfect balance

Game Type: Puzzle Challenge (Into the Breach, Baba Is You, Stephen's Sausage Roll)

Time-to-Solution

  • Tutorial puzzles: 1-3 minutes
  • Early puzzles: 3-10 minutes
  • Mid-game puzzles: 10-30 minutes
  • Late-game puzzles: 30-120 minutes
  • Why these ranges: Cognitive challenge scales with player skill development

Attempt Patterns

  • Solutions requiring discovery: 3-10 attempts to find approach
  • Solutions requiring execution: 1-3 attempts once approach found
  • Aha! moment frequency: Every 3-5 puzzles
  • Why these ranges: Discovery should be challenging, execution confirmatory

Hint System (if present)

  • First hint timing: After 2x expected solve time
  • Hint progression: 3-5 hints from gentle to explicit
  • Hint usage rate: 20-40% of players use hints
  • Why these ranges: Support without trivializing

Challenge + Framework Balance

Game Type: OSR TTRPG (Old School Essentials, Mörk Borg)

Lethality

  • Character death rate: 20-40% chance per session in dangerous situations
  • TPK (total party kill) rate: 5-15% of sessions
  • Death meaning: Permanent (no resurrection)
  • Why these ranges: High stakes create tension, but not so high players won't take risks

Player Agency

  • Creative solution acceptance rate: 60-80% of reasonable proposals
  • Framework consistency: Similar situations → similar rulings (>75% consistency)
  • Player prediction accuracy: 50-70% (uncertainty is feature, not bug)
  • Why these ranges: Rewards creativity without becoming arbitrary

Encounter Difficulty

  • Designed for retreat: 40-60% of encounters can't be won by direct combat
  • Problem-solving emphasis: 3-5 non-combat solutions per major challenge
  • Preparation payoff: Planning reduces risk by 30-50%
  • Why these ranges: Encourages creative play over combat optimization

Resource Scarcity

  • Healing availability: Limited (1-2 full heals per adventure)
  • Ammunition/supplies: Track carefully, scarcity matters
  • Replacement characters: Easy (roll new PC in <10 minutes)
  • Why these ranges: Resources matter without being punishing

Part 2: Mastery Aesthetic Measurables

Mastery + Consistency Balance

Game Type: Deep Deck Builder (Slay the Spire, Monster Train, Griftlands)

Build Diversity

  • Viable archetypes per character: 8-15 distinct strategies
  • Cross-archetype cards: 20-30% work in multiple builds
  • Synergy depth: 3-4 levels of combo chains
  • Why these ranges: Optimization space without overwhelming complexity

Skill Ceiling

  • Novice winrate: 15-30% (learning curve)
  • Experienced winrate: 50-70% (comfortable mastery)
  • Expert winrate: 70-90% (deep optimization)
  • Why these ranges: Clear skill expression, never trivial

Run Variance

  • RNG impact on outcome: 20-35% (skill matters more than luck)
  • Skill can compensate: Expert wins 60%+ of "bad RNG" runs
  • Perfect play not required: Can make 2-3 mistakes and still win
  • Why these ranges: Skill matters but variance prevents determinism

Meta Progression

  • Hours to unlock all content: 30-80 hours
  • Mechanical mastery timeline: 15-30 hours to comfortable
  • Optimization mastery timeline: 80-200 hours to expert
  • Why these ranges: Accessible but deep

Game Type: 4X Strategy (Civilization, Endless Legend)

Strategic Depth

  • Viable strategies: 10-20 distinct approaches
  • Victory condition balance: Each path should be achievable in 30-40% of games
  • Counter-strategy effectiveness: Each strategy has 2-3 strong counters
  • Why these ranges: Rich strategy space without dominant paths

Complexity Management

  • Core systems: 5-10 interconnected mechanics
  • Variables to track: 30-80 simultaneously (with UI support)
  • Decision frequency: Major decisions every 5-10 minutes
  • Why these ranges: Deep without overwhelming (respects cognitive load limits)

Match Duration

  • Quick game mode: 2-4 hours
  • Standard game: 6-12 hours
  • Epic game: 15-30 hours
  • Why these ranges: Various time commitments for different sessions

Skill Expression

  • Novice vs. Expert: 40-60% winrate difference in mirror matches
  • Optimization impact: Expert play 20-30% more efficient
  • Strategic adaptation: 5-8 viable responses to each opponent strategy
  • Why these ranges: Mastery clearly visible

Game Type: Fighting Game (Street Fighter, Guilty Gear)

Execution Barriers

  • Basic combo: 5-8 inputs, 70%+ success rate for intermediate players
  • Intermediate combo: 10-15 inputs, 40-60% success rate
  • Advanced combo: 15-25 inputs, <30% success rate
  • Why these ranges: Execution skill ceiling without gatekeeping

Frame Data

  • Fastest normal attack: 3-5 frames
  • Standard punish window: 6-12 frames
  • Reaction time required: 15-25 frames for intermediate players
  • Why these ranges: Execution matters but isn't unreasonable

Character Balance

  • Tournament viability: 70-85% of roster sees competitive play
  • Top tier advantage: <15% winrate increase over mid tier
  • Character diversity in top 8: 50%+ unique characters
  • Why these ranges: Balance without homogenization

Part 3: Tension Aesthetic Measurables

Tension + Adaptive Balance

Game Type: Co-op Survival Horror (Left 4 Dead, Deep Rock Galactic, GTFO)

Resource Scarcity

  • Health average: 35-55% across team
  • Ammunition average: 30-50% capacity
  • Healing items per player: 1-3 available
  • Why these ranges: Pressure without desperation

Close Call Frequency

  • Individual drops below 20% health: 2-4 times per 10 minutes
  • Team wipe near-misses: 1-2 per session
  • Clutch moments (1 player alive): 0-1 per session (rare but memorable)
  • Why these ranges: Tension peaks create adrenaline

Threat Density

  • Combat encounter frequency: Every 2-4 minutes
  • Safe exploration time: 1-3 minute windows
  • Ambient threat: Always present but manageable
  • Why these ranges: Pressure maintained without exhaustion

Adaptive Adjustment

  • Performance tracking window: 5-10 minute rolling average
  • Adjustment magnitude: ±20-30% from baseline
  • Adjustment speed: Gradual over 2-3 encounters
  • Why these ranges: Invisible adaptation, maintains experience

Death Consequences

  • Individual death: Respawn in 30-90 seconds
  • Team wipe: Restart from last checkpoint (3-8 minutes lost)
  • Permanent failure: Rare (<5% of sessions)
  • Why these ranges: Stakes without frustration

Tension + Consistency Balance

Game Type: Survival Horror (Resident Evil, Alien Isolation)

Resource Scarcity

  • Ammunition capacity: Enough for 30-50% of enemies
  • Healing capacity: 3-5 full heals per area
  • Save tokens: 2-4 per area
  • Why these ranges: Forces conservation and risk evaluation

Threat Lethality

  • Enemy hits to death: 3-5 hits average
  • Critical damage: 50-70% health in single hit (rare)
  • Instant death: Reserved for scripted moments only
  • Why these ranges: Dangerous but not cheap

Safe Space Frequency

  • Safe rooms: Every 10-20 minutes of exploration
  • Save point distance: 5-15 minutes between saves
  • Healing opportunity: Every 8-15 minutes
  • Why these ranges: Tension relief prevents burnout

Encounter Design

  • Avoidable enemies: 40-60% can be bypassed
  • Required combat: 30-40% must be fought
  • Scripted sequences: 10-20% forced encounters
  • Why these ranges: Player agency in risk management

Part 4: Discovery Aesthetic Measurables

Discovery + Framework Balance

Game Type: Investigation TTRPG (Call of Cthulhu, Gumshoe, Brindlewood Bay)

Clue Distribution

  • Core clues: 100% discoverable (automatic or guaranteed)
  • Supporting clues: 60-80% discoverable with thorough investigation
  • Extra clues: 30-50% discoverable with creative approaches
  • Why these ranges: Progress guaranteed, thoroughness rewarded

Investigation Pacing

  • Major revelations: Every 45-90 minutes
  • Minor discoveries: Every 15-30 minutes
  • Dead ends: 20-30% of leads (realism without frustration)
  • Why these ranges: Maintains momentum and discovery feeling

Theory Formation

  • Required clues for solution: 60-70% of total available
  • Red herrings: 10-20% of clues mislead initially
  • Multiple valid theories: 2-3 plausible interpretations until final reveal
  • Why these ranges: Deduction without total certainty

Framework Consistency

  • Investigation action success: 70-85% (emphasis on finding, not failing)
  • Creative approach bonus: +20-30% success for good ideas
  • Framework transparency: Players understand what actions reveal what
  • Why these ranges: Discovery focus over challenge focus

Game Type: Metroidvania (Hollow Knight, Ori, Environmental Station Alpha)

Map Coverage

  • Main path accessibility: 40-60% of map (linear-ish)
  • Optional areas: 30-40% of map (exploration reward)
  • Secret areas: 10-20% of map (dedicated discoverers)
  • Why these ranges: Guided but rewards exploration

Discovery Frequency

  • New areas: Every 15-30 minutes
  • Abilities/upgrades: Every 45-90 minutes
  • Secrets/collectibles: Every 5-15 minutes of exploration
  • Why these ranges: Constant small discoveries, regular big discoveries

Backtracking Value

  • New paths from upgrades: 3-5 per ability
  • Hidden content revealed: 20-30% discovered through backtracking
  • Fast travel availability: After 2-3 hours (respects player time)
  • Why these ranges: Backtracking valuable but not tedious

Part 5: Narrative Aesthetic Measurables

Narrative + Framework Balance

Game Type: Story-Focused TTRPG (Fate, Powered by the Apocalypse, Belonging Outside Belonging)

Character Development

  • Character arcs per campaign: 1-2 major arcs per PC
  • Development milestones: Every 3-5 sessions
  • Mechanical/narrative integration: 70%+ of advancement tied to story
  • Why these ranges: Characters grow meaningfully

Consequence Tracking

  • Choice callback frequency: Major choices referenced 2-4 sessions later
  • Consequence visibility: 60-80% of decisions have visible impact
  • Butterfly effects: 3-5 major branching points per campaign
  • Why these ranges: Choices feel meaningful

Dramatic Pacing

  • Tension/release cycle: Build over 2-3 sessions, climax, release
  • Quiet moments: 20-30% of playtime (character development)
  • Action moments: 30-40% of playtime (plot advancement)
  • Drama moments: 30-40% of playtime (emotional stakes)
  • Why these ranges: Varied pacing maintains engagement

Framework Flexibility

  • Narrative override: GM can adjust mechanics 20-30% for story
  • Player agency: Players drive 60-70% of story direction
  • Prepared content usage: 50-70% of prep gets used (rest adapted)
  • Why these ranges: Responsive to player choices

Narrative + Adaptive Balance

Game Type: Narrative Adventure Game (The Last of Us, Uncharted, God of War)

Story Beat Timing

  • Major plot points: Every 45-90 minutes
  • Character moments: Every 20-30 minutes
  • Combat encounters: Every 10-20 minutes
  • Why these ranges: Pacing maintains story momentum

Difficulty Adaptation

  • Death prevention: Adaptive help after 3-5 deaths on same encounter
  • Story moment guarantee: Critical encounters have safety nets
  • Challenge maintenance: Non-story encounters can be challenging
  • Why these ranges: Story beats land reliably, gameplay not trivialized

Emotional Pacing

  • High intensity: 20-30% of playtime
  • Medium intensity: 40-50% of playtime
  • Low intensity: 20-30% of playtime
  • Why these ranges: Emotional variety prevents exhaustion

Part 6: Submission Aesthetic Measurables

Submission + Adaptive Balance

Game Type: Flow State Game (Vampire Survivors, Peggle, Tetris Effect)

Cognitive Load

  • Simultaneous decisions: 0-3 at any moment
  • Execution complexity: Single input or simple combinations
  • Information tracking: 2-5 variables maximum
  • Why these ranges: "Brain off" relaxation possible

Flow Maintenance

  • Difficulty adjustment speed: Every 30-90 seconds
  • Challenge increase rate: 5-10% per cycle
  • Failure consequence: Minimal (restart quickly, no lost progress)
  • Why these ranges: Maintains flow without breaking it

Session Duration

  • Minimum satisfying session: 5-10 minutes
  • Average session: 15-30 minutes
  • Maximum before fatigue: 45-90 minutes
  • Why these ranges: Accessible for short play, sustainable for long

Skill Expression (Optional)

  • Casual performance baseline: 100% (everyone can play)
  • Skilled performance ceiling: 200-300% (mastery possible but optional)
  • Skill impact on enjoyment: Low (fun at all skill levels)
  • Why these ranges: Accessible with optional depth

Game Type: Cozy Simulation (Animal Crossing, Stardew Valley, Unpacking)

Pressure Levels

  • Time pressure: None or very gentle (no FOMO)
  • Resource pressure: Low (abundance, not scarcity)
  • Failure states: Minimal or absent
  • Why these ranges: Stress-free experience

Progress Pacing

  • Daily achievables: 3-5 meaningful actions per 20-minute session
  • Long-term goals: 10-20 hours to major milestones
  • Completion: 50-100 hours for "everything" (optional)
  • Why these ranges: Steady progress without grind

Decision Complexity

  • Major decisions per session: 2-5 (what to work on)
  • Consequence severity: Very low (all choices valid)
  • Optimization pressure: Optional (can ignore efficiency)
  • Why these ranges: Relaxing decision-making

Part 7: Fellowship Aesthetic Measurables

Fellowship + Framework Balance (Co-op)

Game Type: Cooperative TTRPG (D&D, Blades in the Dark)

Role Differentiation

  • Unique capabilities per character: 60-80% of actions
  • Shared capabilities: 20-40% of actions (cooperation possible)
  • Spotlight distribution: Each player 15-30% of session time
  • Why these ranges: Everyone contributes meaningfully

Cooperation Requirements

  • Challenges requiring teamwork: 40-60% of encounters
  • Solo-able content: 30-40% of encounters
  • Force multiplier from teamwork: 30-50% better outcomes
  • Why these ranges: Cooperation rewarded but not mandatory every moment

Social Dynamics

  • Discussion time: 30-40% of session (planning, debate)
  • Action time: 40-50% of session (execution)
  • Character interaction: 10-20% of session (roleplay)
  • Why these ranges: Balance between social and mechanical play

Fellowship + Adaptive Balance (Co-op Video Game)

Game Type: Co-op Action (Deep Rock Galactic, Helldivers)

Skill Gap Management

  • Performance variance tolerance: Skilled player can carry 1-2 weaker players
  • Carry limit: Can't solo if entire team struggling
  • Adaptive support: Weaker players get minor help (items, respawns)
  • Why these ranges: Keeps group together without removing skill expression

Communication Requirements

  • Mandatory coordination: 20-30% of encounters (must coordinate)
  • Optional coordination: 50-60% of encounters (better together)
  • Individual play: 10-20% of encounters (can split up)
  • Why these ranges: Social game but respects play styles

Failure Recovery

  • Individual death: Revivable by teammates (30-60 seconds)
  • Team wipe: Checkpoint restart (2-5 minutes lost)
  • Permafailure: Rare (<10% of missions)
  • Why these ranges: Stakes without destroying group fun

Part 8: Using This Catalog

Step 1: Find Your Category

  1. Identify primary aesthetic
  2. Identify paradigm
  3. Find game type most similar to yours

Step 2: Select Relevant Measurables

  • Don't use all measurables—choose 3-5 most important
  • Focus on measurables that directly serve your aesthetic
  • Consider your target audience's skill level

Step 3: Set Initial Targets

  • Use catalog values as starting point
  • Adjust for your specific audience (casual = easier end of range)
  • Set concrete numbers (not "approximately")

Step 4: Playtest and Iterate

  • Measure actual values in playtests
  • Compare to targets
  • Adjust parameters ±20% at a time
  • Re-test until convergence

Step 5: Document Your Findings

  • Record final values for your game
  • Note what worked and what didn't
  • Share learnings (helps community)

Appendix A: Conversion Factors

Time Conversions:

  • Micro loop: 5-30 seconds
  • Meso loop: 5-30 minutes
  • Macro loop: 1-6 hours
  • Meta loop: Multiple sessions

Percentage Ranges:

  • Very low: 0-20%
  • Low: 20-40%
  • Medium: 40-60%
  • High: 60-80%
  • Very high: 80-100%

Difficulty Calibration:

  • Casual: 70-90% success rate
  • Normal: 50-70% success rate
  • Hard: 30-50% success rate
  • Expert: 10-30% success rate

Appendix B: When Catalog Values Don't Work

If measurables consistently miss targets:

  1. Check aesthetic alignment - Are you measuring the right things?
  2. Check paradigm fit - Is your paradigm serving your aesthetic?
  3. Check audience calibration - Are targets appropriate for your players?
  4. Check implementation - Are mechanics creating intended dynamics?

Common problems:

  • Too easy: Reduce resources by 20%, increase enemy HP/damage by 15%
  • Too hard: Increase resources by 20%, decrease enemy HP/damage by 15%
  • Too slow: Reduce HP/animation time by 20-30%
  • Too fast: Increase decision complexity (not HP—adds cognitive load)
